## QAV-S 3in crash diagnosis

**Geoff's symptom:** Quad crashes on flips and rolls, NOT on punch-outs or freefall recovery. Happens on any battery, including fresh packs. Video from O4 is shaky.

**Initial hypothesis (wrong):** Propwash / filter tuning. JARFACE proposed dyn_notch and dterm_lpf changes.

**Actual finding from frame data (t=185.8–186.5, full resolution):**

Motor 2 desynced on the re-spin after throttle cut. Sequence:
- t=186.11: throttle cut, all motors drop to minimum
- t=186.13–186.19: m0, m1, m3 spin back up normally; m2 stuck at 48–123 (motor not re-syncing)
- t=186.20+: torque imbalance causes pitch departure (gyroP climbs uncontrolled from 0 to 490°/s)
- FC tries to fight it but can't — m3 eventually pegs at 2047, gyro clips at ±2000°/s (crash)

**Why flips/rolls but not freefall:** Freefall recovery = gentle coordinated spin-up. Flip/roll = hard throttle cut + hard re-spin + simultaneous axis reversal. Desync is triggered by the aggressive re-spin under directional load.

**Why not battery sag:** Motors at 500–1100 before departure, no voltage event. Fresh packs confirm.

**DSHOT600 not applying:** Geoff tried setting DSHOT600, it didn't stick. LUXHDAIO-G4 may be an AIO with integrated ESCs that cap at DSHOT300. Or F4 timer constraint. `get motor_pwm_protocol` after setting will confirm whether it takes. If bidir DSHOT isn't reliable, RPM filter is flying blind.

**Motor resonance present too:** FFT shows dense 80–109 Hz cluster on all axes. dyn_notch_min_hz=90 misses the 80 Hz peak. This is a secondary issue — fix motor 2 first.

## Video shake (O4)

- Live goggle feed: OK (no RockSteady applied, low latency raw)
- Downloaded O4 recording: shaky even before takeoff, worse during maneuvers
- Cause: RockSteady fighting 80–109 Hz motor resonance. With motor 2 rough/desyncy, vibration is unbalanced and harder to correct. On a stiff 3in AIO frame, vibration transmits directly to sensor.
- "Before takeoff" shake: happens if motors are spinning at idle; if motors are completely off and still shaky, camera mount is loose.

## Next steps for Geoff

## Why it matters (JARFACE product angle)

This session validated that JARFACE + analyst subprocess can reach a diagnosis a pilot would never get from just looking at oscillation frequencies. The frame-level desync pattern (m2 lagging on re-spin) is not visible in FFT or aggregate stats — it only shows up in the actual frame trace. This is a real differentiator.
